1. Introduction and data description

Different companies apply different strategies for TV commercial broadcasting, according to their target users/customers and the characteristics of their products. In order to optimize the investment of the TV commercial broadcasting, it is important for a company to quickly understand its current TV commercial broadcasting pattern and compare with its top competitors.


In order to propose a general pipeline to discuss the different patterns in TV commerical broadcast between different brands, I will show an analysis to discuss the three-month (07/2017-10/2017) TV commerical data for 7 automobile brands including Acura, BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Honda, Toyota, Ford and Nissan. All the data is accessible from Alphonso.tv. The original data includes 23 columns, which describes the characteristic of the products, boardcasting time, show and network for each TV commercial of each brands. At last, a general method to discuss any group of brands will be developed.



2 Exploratory data analysis


2.1 Data preprocessing

Data cleaning: Several columns including 'Title','Date','Broadcast Year','Broadcast Quarter','Broadcast Month','Broadcast Week' are dropped, since all the information related to broadcasting time is stored in the 'Time' feature. 'Co-Op Brand' and 'Co-Op Product' have been removed as well due to their high missing value rate. 'Pod Position' is not included in this analysis.


Label encoding: Non-numerical data (except 'Brand') is tranformed by label encoding, while the tranform dictionary is stored for inverse tranformation.


Feature creation: A new feature 'product_year' is created by text-sparsing the title of every TV commericial. If '2016' or '2018' exists in the title, the TV commericial is labeled as 1 or 3, otherwise 2 to represent a current product.

4. Summary

In this analysis, a method to quickly analyze and identify the similarity of the TV commercial airing pattern among different brands is proposed and shown by the discussion on the three-month TV commercial airing data for seven automobile brands. The method basically includes 4 steps:


■ Calculate the aggregate result (the number of airing under different conditions) among different brands to show the general pattern of the TV commercial airing. This also provides a base case for other companies to compare to identify their own advantages or difference. For the seven automobile brands I discussed, the general pattern of TV commercial can be concluded into 4 words: evening, weekend, sports show and male target customers.

■ Use the heapmap of the number of airing to identify the specific pattern on the proposed time resolution. This helps understanding more detailed pattern for each brand and illustrate their difference in TV commercial airing. For example, in the analysis above, the heat map can quickly identify the special time pattern of Mercedes-Benz.

■ Apply the time series of number of airing to show the peak of TV commercial campaign and the transition among old, current and new products.

■ Generate the airing pattern vector for each brand and calculate their similarity coefficients to quickly identify direct competitors (high similarity coefficients) in TV commercial campaign.


This method can be applied for the TV commercial data of any brands or companies, which can help providing more insights through the TV airing data to help the operation of TV commercial campaign for each company.
